Air India Seeks $1.14 Billion Lifeline from SIA and Tata

Air India is seeking a financial infusion of around $1.14 billion (₹9,500 crore) from its parent company Tata Group and partner Singapore Airlines (SIA) to strengthen its balance sheet and fund fleet expansion. The funding aims to support modernization plans, enhance operational efficiency, and reduce debt. The airline is also focusing on upgrading its international services and improving customer experience as part of its ongoing revival strategy and expansion under the Tata-SIA partnership.

2,790 Indians Deported from US in 2025; Crossings Hit 4-Year Low

India Today reported that around 2,790 Indians were deported from the United States in 2025, marking a significant decline in illegal border crossings to a four-year low. U.S. authorities attribute the drop to stricter immigration policies, improved border surveillance, and rising awareness among migrants about the risks of unauthorized entry. Indian officials are coordinating with U.S. agencies to ensure safe repatriation and provide support for citizens affected by the deportation drive.

Kerala Tourism Sees Highest Visitor Surge Post-Pandemic Recovery

Deccan Herald reported that Kerala’s tourism industry has recorded its highest visitor surge since the pandemic, driven by domestic and international travellers returning to the state’s scenic destinations. Popular spots like Munnar, Alappuzha, and Kochi have seen record footfalls. The state government’s new eco-tourism and digital marketing initiatives have boosted global interest, positioning Kerala as one of India’s top post-pandemic travel destinations, contributing significantly to the local economy and hospitality sector.
